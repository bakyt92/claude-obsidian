# Martin Kleppmann

**Role**: Researcher in distributed systems; author of [[Designing Data-Intensive Applications]].

Martin Kleppmann is a computer scientist at the University of Cambridge, best known for writing **DDIA** — one of the most widely-recommended books for backend, data, and platform engineers.

## Why he matters

- **DDIA**: distilled the principles of [[Distributed Systems]] and databases into a single vendor-neutral reference, famous for its clarity and hand-drawn "tree" chapter maps.
- **Research**: works on **CRDTs** (Conflict-free Replicated Data Types) and is a leading voice in the **local-first software** movement — applications that keep data on the user's device and sync peer-to-peer, rather than depending on a central server.
- Previously an industry engineer (startups acquired by LinkedIn) before returning to academia.

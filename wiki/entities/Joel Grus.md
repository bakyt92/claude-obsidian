# Joel Grus

**Role**: Software engineer, data scientist, author and educator.

Joel Grus is best known as the author of [[Data Science from Scratch]] ([[O'Reilly Media]]), the book that teaches data science by implementing every algorithm by hand in pure Python — the canonical example of [[From-Scratch Pedagogy]].

## Why he matters

- **Pedagogy**: his "build it from scratch to understand it" approach has shaped how a generation of self-taught data scientists learned the fundamentals.
- **Research engineering**: has worked at the Allen Institute for AI (AI2) on deep-learning tooling (associated with the AllenNLP library), among other roles.
- **"I Don't Like Notebooks"**: his widely-circulated JupyterCon talk arguing against Jupyter notebooks for serious work — a deliberately provocative critique of reproducibility and engineering hygiene in data science.

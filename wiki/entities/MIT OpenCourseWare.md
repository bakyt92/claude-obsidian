# MIT OpenCourseWare

**What**: MIT's free, open publication of course materials (lecture notes, problem sets, exams, and video). Often abbreviated **OCW**.

OpenCourseWare hosts the archived run and full video of [[MIT 18.06 Linear Algebra]], making [[Gilbert Strang]]'s lectures freely available worldwide. It is the distribution channel that turned 18.06 into a globally-used resource rather than an internal MIT course.

## Why it matters to this vault

- It is the **provenance host** for the 18.06 archive (Spring 2010 materials + Spring 2005 videos).
- Exemplifies the open-education model: immutable archived sources, freely citable — a real-world analogue of this vault's source-first discipline (see [[Source-First Synthesis]]).

# Gilbert Strang

**Role**: Professor of Mathematics, MIT (William Hewlett Professor) · applied mathematician and educator.

Gilbert "Gil" Strang is best known for [[MIT 18.06 Linear Algebra]] and his textbook *Introduction to Linear Algebra*. His lecture videos — distributed free via [[MIT OpenCourseWare]] and YouTube — are among the most-watched mathematics lectures ever recorded.

## Why he matters

- **Pedagogy**: Strang teaches [[Linear Algebra]] geometrically and structurally, leading with the [[Four Fundamental Subspaces]] rather than abstract axioms. This framing is now a standard way the subject is taught.
- **Open education**: an early and prolific contributor to free course materials; 18.06 is a flagship of the OCW catalogue.
- **Applied focus**: wavelets, finite elements, and computational science; he consistently ties linear algebra to applications (least squares, FFT, Markov chains, differential equations).
